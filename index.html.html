Research Philosophy: The "Human-on-a-Chip" Vision

Professor Liang's group operates at the intersection of Chemistry, Engineering, and Biology. The core philosophy drives toward the "Human-on-a-chip" (Body-on-a-chip) paradigm.

Unlike traditional petri dishes, the lab creates micro-environments that mimic the dynamic fluid flow, mechanical stress, and multi-organ interactions of the human body. This technology is then applied to solve complex problems in drug screening and understanding the holistic mechanisms of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM).

Senior Year Transition Strategy

Don't wait until you arrive at Tsinghua. Use your final undergraduate semester to bridge the gap.

Months 1-2: Literature Review

Read Prof. Liang's last 10 papers. Focus on the keywords "Integrated Microfluidics" and "TCM Quality Control". Understand the gap between current tech and biological reality.

Months 3-4: Software Skills

Learn AutoCAD or SolidWorks for chip design. Learn Comsol Multiphysics for fluid simulation. These are the engineering prerequisites.

Arrival: Technique Rotation

Upon arrival, focus on mastering the LC-MS workflow first. It is the analytical backbone of the lab's verification process.
